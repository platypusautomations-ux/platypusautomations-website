Services

Full-Capability Machine Shop

CNC Milling

Multi-axis CNC mill work for complex parts and tight tolerances. Prototype runs to production quantities — held to print every time.

Manual Milling

Bridgeport and manual mill work for one-offs, repairs, and jobs where CNC setup time isn't justified. Fast turnaround on single pieces.

CNC Lathe Work

Precision turning on CNC lathes. Round, threaded, and profiled components machined to exact specification from bar stock or customer-supplied material.

Manual Lathe Work

Manual turning for repairs, custom shafts, bushings, and one-of-a-kind parts. The work other shops won't touch because they don't run manual machines anymore.

Machine Repair

Industrial equipment repair — broken shafts, worn components, failed castings. We reverse-engineer what can't be sourced and make what doesn't exist anymore.

Welding & Fabrication

Structural and precision welding. Custom fabrication from print or sample. MIG, TIG, and stick — steel, stainless, aluminum. In-shop or we come to you.

Marine Repair

Machine work and fabrication for marine applications. Shafting, struts, rudder hardware, custom components. In-shop or on-site at the yard or dock.

Water Jet & Flame Cut

Water jet and flame cutting for plate steel and other materials. Cut to shape, ready for secondary machining or direct-to-fit. Tight tolerances, clean edges.

Material Sales & Consulting

Steel, aluminum, stainless, and specialty materials available. If you need help speccing the right material for the job, we've been answering that question since 1976.

Our Story

Fifty Years.
Same Shop.
Same Standards.

Norfolk Machine & Welding was founded in 1976 by Bobby Mallard, a U.S. Navy veteran with 60 years of experience in the trade. Bobby does things the old school way — because the old school way is right. He built the shop on one principle: do work other shops won't touch, and do it right the first time.

Josephine Mallard brings deep metallurgy knowledge and handles supply purchasing — which means the shop knows exactly what material goes into every job and where it comes from. Pete McCoy brings engineering creativity and expertise that turns problems into workable solutions before they ever reach the floor. Behind them is a crew of long-tenure employees, many with 20 or more years on staff, who are the backbone of every job that leaves this shop.

Call it a mom and pop operation if you want — they would. But the expertise here is specific and named. The customers who come to Norfolk Machine are usually the ones who've already been told no somewhere else. That's fine. That's what we're here for.
