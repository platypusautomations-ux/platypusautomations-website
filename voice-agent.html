The Problem with Missed Night Calls

In the trades, emergencies don't end at 5pm. A burst pipe happens at midnight. A roof leak starts during a thunderstorm. A customer has an urgent problem right now. They call. Your phone goes straight to voicemail. They call your competitor. You never know the job existed until 8am the next day when it's already booked.

If you sleep, you can't answer. If you turn your ringer on, every spam call wakes you. There's no good answer except: don't sleep. Which isn't sustainable.

How After-Hours AI Voice Agent Works

Your phone number rings with a live voice when you're unavailable. The caller hears a greeting in your business's name: "Hi, this is [Your Business]. We're not available right now but our system will help — what's your emergency?" An actual voice. Not a recording. An AI agent that listens, understands, and responds naturally.

For routine calls, the agent says "I'll make sure someone gets back to you first thing in the morning." For emergencies (burst pipe, total power outage, severe weather damage), the agent immediately alerts you. Your phone buzzes with a summary, a transcript, and the caller's number. You can call them back if needed or let it wait until morning.

What You Control

You define what qualifies as an "emergency" in your business. For plumbers, a burst pipe is urgent. For roofers, a leak during a rainstorm is urgent. You set the criteria, and the AI learns. Non-emergency calls — routine questions, information requests, or calls that come back to you after hours — get logged, but you don't get woken up.

The Morning Brief

You wake up to a list of all after-hours calls. Hot prospects flagged with full details. Routine calls logged. You prioritize from your dashboard. Call the emergencies first. Follow up on the rest during business hours. You've just captured 8 hours of leads while you slept.

Peace of Mind

You know your phone is answered. Callers don't get dead air or a generic voicemail. They get treated like they matter — because they do. Your business feels bigger, more professional, and always on. And you get to sleep.

Common Questions

After-Hours Voice Agent FAQ

Does the AI sound like a robot?
No. Modern voice AI sounds natural and conversational — close enough that most callers don't realize they're not talking to a human until the handoff. The greeting uses your business name and professional tone. Callers take it seriously.
What if a caller demands to speak to a human?
The AI can offer to take a message, confirm their callback number, and escalate immediately if they claim it's an emergency. No confrontation. The AI handles edge cases smoothly because it's designed for them.
How do I know what qualifies as an emergency?
You define it with us before launch. For plumbing, burst pipes and no water are emergency. For roofing, active leaks and storm damage are emergency. For HVAC, total system down in winter is emergency. You set the rules, the AI applies them.
What if I'm not actually available at night?
That's the point. The system logs and prioritizes. You decide if you want to answer emergencies or just review them in the morning. Either way, the caller is handled professionally, not left hanging.
Does this replace my voicemail?
Yes and better. The AI takes a message, but it's smarter — it asks follow-up questions, captures structured data, and flags urgency. No more garbled voicemails or missed details. Everything is clear in your dashboard.
Can I use this during business hours too?
Yes. Some clients run it 24/7 so every call is screened before it reaches them, day and night. Handles after-hours automatically and helps during peak hours by pre-qualifying all inbound calls.
