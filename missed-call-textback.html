The Problem with Missed Calls

A customer calls your business. Your phone rings three times. You're on a job site, in the middle of service, or away from your desk. The call goes to voicemail. The customer hears a generic greeting, hangs up, and calls the next business on Google. You'll never know the job existed.

62% of customers who reach voicemail do not leave a message. They move on. In the trades, every missed call is a lost job — sometimes a $5,000 or $10,000 opportunity gone in seconds because you didn't answer fast enough.

How Missed Call Text-Back Works

The moment a call goes unanswered, our system immediately sends an SMS to that number. The message is personalized to your business. It arrives within 60 seconds, before the customer has finished deciding whether to call someone else.

The text says: "Hi! This is [Your Business]. Sorry we missed you — we're helping another customer right now but we got your number and will call you back ASAP." The lead stays warm. The customer knows they were heard.

What Happens Next

Your system logs the inbound call with the customer's number, call time, and our automated response. You get notified instantly (email or text) that someone called. You see the lead in your dashboard. You call them back while you're still top of mind — not tomorrow, not after 5pm, but now. That's the difference between a booked job and a lost customer.

Why Speed Matters in the Trades

A customer with an emergency (burst pipe, HVAC down, roof leak) has a narrow window where they're trying to solve the problem today. The first contractor to respond often gets the job. A missed call + delayed callback = a customer who booked your competitor 20 minutes ago. A missed call + 60-second text-back = a customer who sees you're real, responsive, and deserves a second call.

Personalization & Brand Voice

We write the text message in your voice, using your business name. It doesn't say "Automated System" or read like a robot. It reads like someone from your office who cares that they missed the call. Small detail, huge impact on perception.

Common Questions

Missed Call Text-Back FAQ

What if my phone number is already on a "do not call" list?
Missed call text-back is an inbound response to a call the customer initiated — not an outbound marketing blast. It falls under transactional messaging, not marketing, and is not subject to DNC list restrictions. The customer called you first. Our response is just acknowledging that call.
Can I customize the message?
Yes. We write the initial template to match your voice and business name. You can request changes to the message, and we'll implement them. The message stays consistent so customers recognize it as coming from your business.
What if a customer texts back?
If they reply to the automated message, their text comes to your phone and your system logs it. You can respond directly. The conversation then becomes a normal back-and-forth between you and the customer.
Does this work for calls from robots or spam?
Yes — the system responds to every inbound call, including robocalls. However, most spam doesn't trigger a response because it's a recorded message or click-to-call ad, not a real person. In your lead log, the spam calls are obvious and you can ignore them. Real customer calls stand out.
Can I set hours when the auto-text fires?
Yes. We can configure it to respond only during business hours, only after-hours, or 24/7. Most clients keep it on all the time because missed calls happen anytime — evenings, weekends, while you're on another job.
Does this replace my voicemail?
No. Voicemail stays as a backup. The text-back fires immediately while voicemail is optional for the caller. Most customers get the text and wait for your call — they don't leave a message. You get both the text notification and the voicemail if they left one.
