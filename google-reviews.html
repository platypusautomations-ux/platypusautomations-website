The Review Gap

You've been in business 10 years. You've done 500 jobs. You have 12 Google reviews. Meanwhile, a competitor with half your experience has 150 reviews and ranks above you in Google Maps. Why? They asked. You didn't. Or you asked inconsistently. They systematized it. You didn't.

Google ranks visibility on review velocity (how many new reviews you get, how often). A business that gets 2-3 reviews per month ranks faster than a business that gets 2-3 per year, even if the 10-year business has better work.

How Review Automation Works

After every job closes, we send a Google review request to the customer. Not an email they ignore. A text message or email with a direct link to leave a review on Google — no login required. Customers click, leave a star rating and comment, and it posts. 30% of customers who receive a direct review request actually leave one. Without the request, that percentage drops to 1-2%.

The Math

If you close 10 jobs a month and ask for reviews on each one, you get 3 new reviews per month on average. In 12 months, that's 36 new reviews. In 3 months, you're at 9. Your Google ranking shifts. The algorithm notices momentum. Competitors with stagnant review counts can't keep up.

AI Review Responses

Every review — 5-star or 1-star — gets a professional response written by AI. Not canned. Not your voice. Professional acknowledgment. Thank-yous on positive reviews. Problem-solving on negative ones. It shows Google (and future customers) that you engage with feedback. Engaged businesses rank higher.

Timeline

Common Questions

Google Reviews FAQ

Can Google penalize me for asking for reviews?
No. Google encourages review requests. What they penalize is fake reviews, incentivized reviews (paying for good reviews), and fake negative reviews of competitors. Asking real customers for honest reviews is 100% compliant and encouraged by Google.
What if customers leave negative reviews?
Negative reviews happen. Our AI responds professionally to every review, including negative ones. A thoughtful response to a negative review shows Google and future customers that you engage and care about feedback. Often, customers change their rating after a good response.
When should I ask for a review?
Immediately after a job closes, while the customer's experience is fresh and positive. The first 48 hours are golden. We send requests on your schedule — day after job completion, 3 days after, or whenever you define.
Do I respond to reviews myself?
Our AI writes responses automatically. You can review and approve them if you like, or let them post automatically. Most clients set it to automatic so there's no delay — faster responses rank better in Google's algorithm.
How fast will my ranking improve?
Most clients see improvements in 60–90 days. The more reviews you get in that window, the faster the change. Businesses that get 2-3 reviews per month see faster rank jumps than those getting one per quarter.
Does this work for all trade types?
Yes. Roofing, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, junk removal, tree service — all benefit from higher review counts. Any trade where customers search Google Maps benefits from aggressive review collection.
